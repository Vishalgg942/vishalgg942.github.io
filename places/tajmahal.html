Taj Mahal

Agra, Uttar Pradesh

4.9 ★ World Heritage Site

The Epitome of Love

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Key Features

  • Built between 1632-1653
  • Combines Islamic, Persian and Indian architectural styles
  • Made with white marble from Makrana, Rajasthan
  • Recognized as UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983

Visitor Information

Timings: Sunrise to Sunset (Closed Fridays)

Entry Fee: ₹50 for Indians, ₹1100 for foreigners

Best Time to Visit: October to March
